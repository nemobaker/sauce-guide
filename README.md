# sauce-guide

# 🐟🍟 The Fish & Chips Definitive Sauce Guide

*(working title, also final title, this is the definitive guide after all)*

## What is this

A highly scientific, peer-reviewed-by-nobody database of every sauce, condiment,
paste, dip, gravy, and questionable liquid known to humankind — and whether it is
permitted within 10 feet of a piece of fried fish.

There are two judges. Their rulings are final, arbitrary, and occasionally
contradictory:

- **🐟 Nemo** — discerning, has Opinions, once declared a fish sauce "fine if
  it's mixed into something else" which is a very fish-sauce thing to say about
  fish sauce
- **🍪 Chipper** — generally agreeable, draws the line firmly at funky cheese

## The verdicts, decoded

| Badge | Meaning |
|---|---|
| ✅ Yes | Cleared for duty |
| ❌ No | Rejected, do not pass the chips |
| 🤔 Unsure | The committee is still in session and has yet to try the sauce in question |
| 🟡 Maybe | Depends on mood, lunar cycle, who's asking |
| No* | No, but with a footnote, because nuance exists even in condiment law |

## Notable rulings

- Ketchup: **no**, in all four(!!) of its documented forms. The committee finds
  it suspicious that there are this many ketchups.
- Compote: yes, but **not apples**. Specifics matter.
- Cream: under deep philosophical review — *"is cream really a sauce?"* — a
  question that may never be settled.
- A1 Steak Sauce: the **only** acceptable steak sauce. "The classic." No
  substitutes will be entertained.

## How to use this site

Search for a sauce. Learn its fate. Add one we missed (there's apparently
always one more relish-adjacent Eastern European condiment we haven't ruled
on yet). Your additions live in your browser only — nothing leaves this page,
nothing is tracked, no sauce data is sold to third parties.
